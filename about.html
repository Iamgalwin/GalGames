ABOUT ME

Hi, I’m Galwin Joy, and video games have been a huge part of my life for as long as I can remember. For me, gaming has never been just a way to kill time. It’s something I genuinely enjoy and feel connected to. Games are more than just entertainment — they’re stories, challenges, and whole worlds to explore. Over time, gaming has also taught me a lot of things like patience, problem-solving, and sometimes even how to deal with failure after losing the same level ten times in a row.

My interest in games actually started long before I owned my first laptop. Back then, having a device to play games on felt like a distant dream. I used to watch gameplay videos, read about games, and imagine what it would feel like to actually play them myself. So when I finally got my first laptop, it honestly felt like a dream coming true. It wasn’t a powerful gaming machine or anything fancy, but it didn’t matter — it was my ticket into the gaming world I had been thinking about for years.

Gaming also helped me during times when I didn’t really have many friends around to spend time with. Instead of just feeling bored or disconnected, games gave me something to focus on and enjoy. Exploring different game worlds, completing missions, and learning new mechanics gave me a sense of purpose and kept my mind active. Over time, gaming became something I’m genuinely passionate about, and it’s still something I enjoy deeply today.

And honestly… I might be a little too passionate about games. I can start a game “just for 10 minutes” and suddenly it’s 3 AM and I’m still saying “okay one more mission.” I’ve probably restarted games a hundred times just because I wanted the perfect start. Sometimes I spend more time tweaking settings and controls than actually playing the game. And yes, I’ve definitely blamed lag, my controller, and sometimes even the universe for my bad gameplay. But that’s part of the fun. Gaming is chaotic, frustrating, exciting, and sometimes completely ridiculous — and that’s exactly why I love it.
